Leavell & Clark's Framework

Health education and public health interventions cover a continuum known as Leavell's Levels of Prevention. This framework divides prevention into distinct phases based on the natural history of a disease.

Primary Prevention

Health Promotion
+
Specific Protection

Action taken before the disease occurs.

Early Diagnosis
+
Prompt Treatment

Action taken to halt the disease process early.

Disability Limitation
+
Rehabilitation

Action taken to restore function and prevent complications.

Primordial & Primary Prevention

Primordial Prevention

Focus: Preventing the development of the risk factors themselves in the general population.

Examples (Often involves public policy & societal changes):
  • Banning junk food advertisements targeted at children.
  • National policies to create safe bike lanes to prevent sedentary lifestyles.
  • Preventing the initiation of smoking among the youth.

Primary Prevention

Focus: Preventing the disease before it occurs. The target population is healthy individuals.

Examples (Health Promotion & Specific Protection):
  • Immunization / Vaccination (EPI Program).
  • Health education on healthy lifestyle and breastfeeding.
  • Environmental sanitation and handwashing.
  • Smoking cessation campaigns.
  • Wearing seatbelts and helmets.

Secondary & Tertiary Prevention

Secondary Prevention

Focus: Early diagnosis and prompt treatment to halt disease progression.

Target: People who are largely asymptomatic but have risk factors, or those in the very early stages of disease.

Examples (Screening & Case Finding):
  • Screening for hypertension, diabetes, and cancer.
  • Pap smear and Breast Self-Examination (BSE).
  • Case finding for TB and leprosy.
  • Prenatal check-ups.

Tertiary Prevention

Focus: Disability limitation and rehabilitation to restore function.

Target: People with an established, symptomatic, or chronic disease.

Examples (Restoration & Complication Prevention):
  • Physical therapy for stroke patients.
  • Foot care for diabetics to prevent amputation.
  • Community-Based Rehabilitation for PWDs.
  • Home care for the chronically ill or dying.
